# Evidence Card

## Card ID

`SOURCE-YEAR-F#`

## Source

Full citation, link, archive path, or retrieval note.

## Finding

One sentence stating only what was measured or observed.

Do not include causal interpretation unless the study directly tested causation.

## Author Interpretation

Quote or closely paraphrase what the authors claimed the finding means.

## Method Type

Choose one:

- observational
- quasi-experimental
- experimental
- simulation
- qualitative
- meta-analysis
- anecdotal / administrative
- other

## Population / Scope

System, population, time window, setting, and conditions covered.

## Effect Size And Uncertainty

Numbers if available. If not available, write `not reported`.

## Direction Of Support

List each theory touched by the finding.

| Theory | Supports / undercuts / neutral / scope-limits | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
|  |  |  |

## Strength Grade

Use a simple 1-4 scale:

1. anecdote, administrative data, or unverified practitioner claim
2. correlational or descriptive empirical evidence
3. controlled comparison, natural experiment, strong quasi-experimental design, or replicated observational result
4. randomized, replicated controlled result, or unusually strong convergent evidence

## Conflicts With

List card IDs this finding appears to contradict. If none are known, write `none known`.

## Funding / Incentive Note

Who funded the work? Did any funder, author, vendor, institution, or agency have a stake in the answer?

## Status

Choose one:

- active
- superseded
- retracted
- replication-pending
- unclear

## Reviewer Check

- [ ] Finding sentence separates observation from interpretation.
- [ ] Scope is explicit.
- [ ] Conflict field is populated.
- [ ] Strength grade is justified.
- [ ] Source is retrievable.
