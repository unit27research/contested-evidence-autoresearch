# Contested Evidence Autoresearch

## A Field Kit for Domains Without Clean Scoreboards

Autoresearch systems work best when there is a clean feedback signal. A loop can search, edit, test, and improve when the environment can answer in a usable way: the benchmark moved, the simulation passed, the experiment ran, the loss curve improved, the test failed.

Contested evidence fields are different. They often lack a clean scoreboard. Studies disagree for reasons that are not always obvious. Different teams measure different constructs with the same words. Findings shift across populations, time periods, and methods. Some claims are supported by many citations but only one underlying dataset. Some theories survive because no one has written down what would undercut them.

In those settings, optimizing for a confident answer can make the system worse. The useful target is not early convergence. The useful target is better disagreement.

This field kit proposes an anti-convergence loop for unresolved domains. Anti-convergence does not mean refusing to decide. It means refusing to collapse uncertainty before the evidence supports it.

## Intended Use

This kit is for teams that need to reason across mixed evidence and competing theories:

- research teams comparing causal explanations
- policy or strategy teams deciding what evidence to collect next
- investigative researchers building source-backed claim maps
- review teams trying to separate findings from interpretations
- analysts working in fields where consensus language hides unresolved scope questions

It is useful when the next responsible move is not a conclusion, but a better map of the disagreement.

It is not useful when the problem has a clean empirical test, a validated benchmark, a formal review protocol that should already govern the work, or an urgent safety-critical decision path. In those cases, use the stronger method.

## The Problem

Many automated research loops inherit a simple shape:

1. generate an idea
2. test it
3. score the result
4. keep the improvement
5. repeat

That shape fits domains with a measurable objective. It is weaker in domains where the disputed object is itself unclear: the outcome definition, population, scope condition, causal mechanism, or standard of evidence.

In a contested field, a loop that searches for the strongest answer may learn to prefer:

- clean narratives over unresolved conflict
- high-volume citation clusters over independent support
- theory-preserving interpretations over scope limits
- apparent consensus over visible disagreement
- confidence language over source discipline

The failure mode is not only technical. It is social. Teams converge because convergence is comfortable.

## Proposed Response

The loop should optimize the condition of the evidence structure before it optimizes the answer.

The core artifacts are:

- evidence cards
- study autopsies
- competing-theory pages
- diagnostic theory rubrics
- adversarial review briefs
- mechanically generated research questions
- source-collection coverage matrices

Each artifact feeds the next. Cards feed autopsies. Autopsies update theory pages. Theory pages feed rubrics. Rubric splits and unresolved autopsies generate questions. Questions define the next collection plan. New sources create new cards.

The loop is useful when it makes the field harder to oversimplify.

## Minimum First Cycle

A team does not need to run the full framework on day one.

The smallest useful cycle is:

1. write two to four competing theory pages
2. create at least five evidence cards
3. run one autopsy on the clearest source conflict
4. generate three research questions from the artifacts
5. write a short cycle report naming what changed, what stayed unresolved, and what evidence should be collected next

If that first cycle produces no conflicts, no undercutting evidence, and no scope limits, the likely problem is the collection process, not the field.

## Evidence Cards

Each source produces one card per finding. A study with four findings produces four cards. This prevents a study from entering the system as one rhetorical object.

The card separates:

- the observed finding
- the author's interpretation
- the method
- the population and scope
- the effect size and uncertainty
- the theory or theories touched
- the strength grade
- known conflicts
- funding or incentive notes
- status

The most important rule is that contradicted findings are not deleted. A contradicted card remains in the deck with the conflict recorded. In a contested field, contradiction is not housekeeping debris. It is part of the object being studied.

## Study Autopsies

When credible sources disagree, the loop runs an autopsy instead of selecting the preferred citation.

The autopsy asks why the studies disagree:

- different constructs
- different populations or conditions
- different time periods
- methodological artifact
- analytic flexibility
- likely spurious result
- genuine unresolved conflict

The ordering matters. Teams often jump to methodological dismissal too early. This loop checks construct, scope, and time before accusing a study of being wrong.

The most valuable output is the genuine unresolved conflict. That conflict becomes a research question.

## Theory Pages

Each competing theory gets the same template:

- causal claim
- mechanism
- core predictions
- distinctive predictions
- supporting cards
- undercutting cards
- scope conditions
- anomalies
- compatibility notes

The empty undercutting section is treated as a warning sign. It may mean the theory is strong. It may also mean no one has tried to make it vulnerable.

## Diagnostic Rubric

The rubric scores theories on dimensions such as explanatory coverage, distinctive prediction record, mechanism specificity, independence of support, and anomaly honesty.

The score is not a verdict. It is a diagnostic. A theory can be strong on coverage and weak on independent support. Another can be narrow but mechanistically specific. Those differences matter more than a total score.

Reviewer disagreement is recorded, not averaged away. A split score is evidence that the team disagrees about the evidence.

## Adversarial Review

The adversarial process makes disagreement a role, not a personality trait.

Each cycle assigns rotating roles:

- theory advocate
- designated dissenter
- autopsy challenger
- scope auditor

The dissenter briefs the leading theory before the advocate. Scope auditors rewrite broad claims into claims that match the evidence. Any reviewer can file a convergence alarm when the process records no meaningful disagreement across repeated meetings.

This is the metacognitive part of the loop. It watches the reasoning system, not only the sources.

## Research-Question Generation

Questions are generated from the artifacts:

- every genuine unresolved conflict
- every untested distinctive prediction
- every empty undercutting section
- every split rubric score
- every scope-auditor flag

Then questions are prioritized by discrimination power, feasibility, and confidence leverage.

The top question is not the most interesting question. It is the question most likely to change the relative standing of competing theories.

## Source Collection

Collection is guided by a matrix of theory by method type by time period.

The next source sprint targets thin cells, not comfortable cells. If a theory already has twelve correlational cards and no controlled comparisons, the next collection pass looks for controlled comparisons.

Each cycle reserves effort for adversarial collection: searching against the leading theory and for the weakest plausible theory. The search terms and sources are recorded.

Saturation is recorded per cell. The framework never permits a broad claim that the team has read everything.

## What The Loop Optimizes

The loop optimizes:

- source traceability
- disagreement quality
- claim scope
- conflict visibility
- theory vulnerability
- next-question value

It does not optimize:

- confidence for its own sake
- citation volume
- rhetorical neatness
- a single winning theory
- automated authority

## Worked Example

The fictional Carverton transit example shows how the framework behaves when four theories compete:

- schedule fragility
- maintenance debt
- governance churn
- demand mismatch

One apparent conflict dissolves after the autopsy shows the studies measured different incident thresholds. Another conflict survives and becomes a research question. The governance theory begins with no undercutting cards, which triggers adversarial review and a sharper prediction.

The cycle ends with a modest result: schedule fragility and maintenance debt may both be partly true under different mechanism steps, while one conflict remains open. That is not a failure. It is a better map of the field.

## Novelty Boundary

This framework does not invent evidence synthesis, systematic review, argument mapping, claim verification, literature-based discovery, or agentic research loops.

Its contribution is an operating synthesis: a manual-first loop that adapts autoresearch thinking to fields where the useful target is not a clean objective score but a more inspectable disagreement structure.

The claim should stay modest. This is a field kit for careful reasoning, not a new scientific authority.
