# Carverton Evidence Cards

## CARV-2019-F2

Source: Carverton Transit Reliability Study, 2019.

Finding: Delays longer than ten minutes on upstream rail segments were followed by downstream delay propagation in 64 percent of observed weekday peak incidents.

Author interpretation: The authors argued that Carverton's timetable lacks enough slack to absorb major upstream incidents.

Method type: quasi-experimental

Population / scope: Rail network, weekday peak periods, 2017-2018.

Effect size and uncertainty: 64 percent propagation rate; confidence interval not reported in this fictional example.

Direction of support:

| Theory | Direction | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| T1 Schedule Fragility | supports | Supports cascade mechanism for incidents over ten minutes. |
| T2 Maintenance Debt | neutral | Does not identify cause of upstream incidents. |

Strength grade: 3

Conflicts with: CARV-2020-F1, apparent conflict only

Funding / incentive note: City planning office.

Status: active

## CARV-2020-F1

Source: Carverton Incident Frequency Review, 2020.

Finding: Daily incident count was not correlated with system-wide average delay across the full incident log.

Author interpretation: The authors argued that incident frequency does not explain Carverton's reliability problem.

Method type: observational

Population / scope: Bus and rail incident logs, all recorded incidents, 2018-2019.

Effect size and uncertainty: Correlation near zero; exact coefficient not reported in this fictional example.

Direction of support:

| Theory | Direction | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| T1 Schedule Fragility | appears to undercut | Only undercuts if incident count is the right construct. |

Strength grade: 3

Conflicts with: CARV-2019-F2, apparent conflict only

Funding / incentive note: Transit agency operations division.

Status: active

## CARV-2021-F1

Source: Carverton Asset Reliability Audit, 2021.

Finding: Vehicles above the fleet median age had 2.4 times more service-removal events than vehicles below the fleet median age.

Author interpretation: The authors argued that deferred maintenance is the main driver of service unreliability.

Method type: observational

Population / scope: Bus fleet service-removal records, 2016-2020.

Effect size and uncertainty: 2.4x; uncertainty not reported in this fictional example.

Direction of support:

| Theory | Direction | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| T2 Maintenance Debt | supports | Supports asset-age mechanism. |
| T1 Schedule Fragility | compatible | Maintenance failures may trigger schedule cascades. |

Strength grade: 3

Conflicts with: none known

Funding / incentive note: Independent audit commissioned by city council.

Status: active

Effect size and uncertainty: Corridor-level ridership gains; uncertainty not reported in this fictional example.
