# Carverton Study Autopsies

## AUTOPSY-2026-001

Conflicting cards:

- CARV-2019-F2
- CARV-2020-F1

Apparent conflict:

One study found that long upstream rail incidents were followed by downstream delay propagation. Another found no correlation between total daily incident count and system-wide average delay.

Taxonomy:

| Category | Fits? | Reasoning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A. Different constructs | yes | One study measures propagation after long incidents. The other counts all incidents, including short incidents. |
| B. Different populations or conditions | partial | Rail peak periods differ from full bus and rail logs. |
| C. Different time periods | no | Time windows overlap enough that timing does not explain the full conflict. |
| D. Methodological artifact | not needed | The construct difference explains the apparent conflict first. |
| E. Analytic flexibility | possible | Different aggregation choices may matter, but they are not the first explanation. |
| F. Likely spurious result | no | Both cards remain active. |
| G. Genuine unresolved conflict | no | The apparent conflict dissolves into a scope condition. |

Assigned category: A. Different constructs.

Reasoning:

Incident count is not the same construct as cascade propagation from long incidents. T1 should be narrowed to incidents over a duration threshold rather than defended as a claim about all incidents.

What would resolve this:

Partition incident logs by duration and mode, then test whether propagation appears above a threshold.

## AUTOPSY-2026-002

Conflicting cards:

- CARV-2022-F3
- CARV-2023-F1

Apparent conflict:

One source found off-network demand growing despite improved frequency. Another found ridership recovery where improved frequency was paired with direct cross-town routes.

Taxonomy:

| Category | Fits? | Reasoning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A. Different constructs | partial | Off-network demand and corridor ridership recovery are related but not identical. |
| B. Different populations or conditions | yes | Frequency-only improvements differ from frequency plus route redesign. |
| C. Different time periods | partial | The pilot follows the survey period. |
| D. Methodological artifact | possible | The 2023 memo is administrative and lower grade. |
| E. Analytic flexibility | possible | Corridor selection may shape results. |
| F. Likely spurious result | not enough evidence | The lower-grade memo should not be treated as decisive. |
| G. Genuine unresolved conflict | partial | The scope condition is plausible but not settled. |

Assigned category: B with remaining G.

Reasoning:

The best current explanation is that frequency-only improvements and frequency-plus-route-redesign are different interventions. Still, the lower strength of the 2023 card leaves unresolved uncertainty.

What would resolve this:

Compare corridors where frequency improved with and without route-shape changes, using the same ridership and demand measures.
