# Carverton Cycle Report

Cycle: fictional v0.1 example

## Summary

The first Carverton pass does not identify a winning theory. It improves the structure of the disagreement.

Schedule fragility and maintenance debt appear compatible. Maintenance failures may create incidents, while schedule fragility may amplify the consequences of longer incidents. Governance churn remains plausible but under-vulnerable. Demand mismatch gains a narrower scope condition: frequency improvements may fail when route shape does not match current trips.

## What Changed

The apparent conflict between CARV-2019-F2 and CARV-2020-F1 was narrowed. The studies measured different constructs: cascade propagation after long incidents versus total incident count.

T1 was revised from a broad claim about incidents to a narrower claim about incidents over a duration threshold.

T3 was flagged because no undercutting cards have been filed.

T4 was not rejected by CARV-2023-F1. The conflict created a scope question about frequency-only improvements versus frequency plus route redesign.

## Open Conflicts

- Whether maintenance debt independently explains failures after controlling for schedule density.
- Whether governance churn has a real undercutting condition.
- Whether demand mismatch depends on route shape, not only service frequency.

## Next Questions

2. RQ-2026-002: Identify observations that would undercut governance churn.
3. RQ-2026-003: Compare frequency-only corridors with frequency-plus-redesign corridors.
