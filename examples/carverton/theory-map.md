## T1 Schedule Fragility

Claim: Carverton fails because the timetable has too little slack, so major upstream delays cascade through the system.

Mechanism:

1. A delay crosses a duration threshold.
2. The timetable lacks recovery slack.
3. Downstream vehicle and crew assignments slip.
4. System-wide reliability falls.

Distinctive prediction: Delay propagation should be stronger after long upstream incidents than after short incidents.

Supporting cards: CARV-2019-F2

Undercutting cards: CARV-2020-F1, apparent only after autopsy

Scope condition: The cascade mechanism applies most clearly to rail incidents longer than ten minutes.

Compatibility: Compatible with T2 if maintenance failures create the upstream incidents.

## T2 Maintenance Debt

Claim: Carverton fails because deferred upkeep increases service-removal events and asset-related disruptions.

Mechanism:

1. Aging assets receive insufficient preventive maintenance.
2. Older assets fail more often in service.
3. Service removals reduce available fleet capacity.
4. Reliability and ridership suffer.

Distinctive prediction: Failures cluster on older assets even after controlling for route schedule density.

Supporting cards: CARV-2021-F1

Undercutting cards: none known

Scope condition: Stronger for fleet-dependent bus service than for governance-driven reform outcomes.

Compatibility: Compatible with T1 if equipment failures trigger cascading delays.

## T3 Governance Churn

Claim: Carverton fails because leadership turnover interrupts reform implementation before changes can mature.

Mechanism:

1. Reform is launched.
2. Executive or board leadership changes.
3. Implementation priorities shift.
4. Milestones slip or are abandoned.

Distinctive prediction: Similar reform designs should differ in outcome based on whether sponsoring leadership remained through implementation.

Supporting cards: CARV-2017-F4

Undercutting cards: none filed, flag

Scope condition: Applies to reform implementation, not necessarily to day-to-day equipment failure.

Compatibility: Compatible with T1, T2, and T4 as an explanation for why fixes fail to persist.

## T4 Demand Mismatch

Claim: Carverton fails because service improvements do not match current trip patterns.

Mechanism:

1. Travel demand shifts.
2. Transit routes remain oriented around older commute patterns.
3. Frequency improvements miss actual trips.
4. Riders use other modes despite service investment.

Distinctive prediction: Off-network demand should grow even where frequency improves, unless route shape changes.

Supporting cards: CARV-2022-F3

Undercutting cards: CARV-2023-F1

Scope condition: Frequency-only improvements may fail; frequency plus route redesign may recover ridership.

Compatibility: Compatible with T3 if governance churn prevents route redesign.
